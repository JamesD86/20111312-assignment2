The Shawshank Redemption
1994
Based on Stephen King's "Rita Hayworth and the Shawshank Redemption", this masterpiece tells the story of a convicted murderer, Andy Dufresne, as he clings to hope for survival in a brutal maximum security prison. Corruption, murder, and grief are abound, but offset by friendship with an unlikely co-conspirator, Red. This film is widely regarded as the gretest ever made, despite being a theatrical flop when released.

Lord of the Rings
2001-2003
Based on the genius works of J.R.R. Tolkien, the Lord of the Rings trilogy is widely considered to be among the great works of fantasy every adapted for the screen. Director Peter Jackson brings the world of Middle Earth to life in an epic adventure that spans more than 12 hours if watched back to back. I consider myself privileged to have seen all three in theater when released.

The Godfather II
1974
Directed by Francis Ford Coppola, and considered his masterpiece, the Godfather II picks up where its predecessor left off. Michael Corleone, portrayed by Al Pacino, devolves from a decorated war hero to the ruthless head of his Mafia crime family. Michael's neverending thirst to rid the enemies of his crime family, tears him from his real family, and he sinks deeper into the darkness.
